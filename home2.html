Recycling isn’t optional
It’s survival

On Mars, every piece of waste is a chance to reuse and survive. Recycling isn’t optional, it’s essential.

Textile Waste

Clothing

During a Mars mission, astronauts rely on specialized clothing like flight suits, thermal layers, socks, and gloves for protection, temperature control, and hygiene. Over time, these garments wear out, become damaged, or lose effectiveness — turning into bulky textile waste.

Instead of discarding used clothing, crews must find ways to repurpose it into useful materials like insulation, cleaning cloths, or patch kits to reduce reliance on Earth-supplied resources.

Flight suits

Worn during launch and landing for safety. They wear out and become trash over time.

Gloves & socks

Protect hands and feet during daily tasks. They get dirty or torn and turn into waste.

Blankets & wraps

Used to keep astronauts warm and protect gear. Over time, they tear or wear out and become trash.

Thermal covers

Used to shield equipment from heat and cold. Once damaged or replaced, they turn into waste.

Insulation Fabrics

Insulation fabrics are used to protect astronauts and equipment from extreme Mars temperatures and dust. They line sleeping areas, wrap sensitive gear, and cover habitat walls. Over time, these materials wear out, tear, or get replaced — turning into bulky textile waste.

Instead of discarding them, crews must repurpose insulation fabrics into thermal padding, equipment wraps, or wall reinforcements to reduce waste and extend mission resources.

Straps & Belts

Straps and belts are used throughout a Mars mission to secure equipment, fasten gear, and stabilize supplies in low gravity. They’re essential for safety and organization inside the habitat and during transport. Over time, these items can fray, lose strength, or break — turning into durable textile waste.

Instead of discarding them, crews must find ways to reuse straps and belts as tie-downs, tension supports, or DIY harnesses to reduce waste and extend mission resources.

Gear harnesses

Gear harnesses hold tools and supplies so they don’t float away in space. Over time, they get worn out and become trash. Find ways to use old harnesses again to save materials and reduce waste.

Tie-downs

Used to keep equipment and supplies secure in low gravity. When worn out, they can be reused to help hold things in place and reduce waste.
