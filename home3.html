Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun, and the seventh largest. It’s the only planet we know of inhabited entirely by robots.

Structural Waste

Protective Structures

These parts shield the spacecraft and habitat from heat, dust, and impact. Items like heat shields, dust covers, and impact panels are critical during landing and setup. But once they’re burned, torn, or no longer needed, they become bulky trash that must be reused or repurposed.

Heat Shield

Heat Shields protect the spacecraft from extreme heat during entry into the Martian atmosphere. They absorb intense friction and often burn or crack, making them unusable after landing. Because they cannot be repaired or reused, heat shields become heavy, damaged waste left on the surface.

Dust Covers

Dust Covers shield equipment from the fine and pervasive Martian dust that can clog and damage sensitive instruments. Over time, these covers tear or wear out due to harsh conditions, losing their protective ability and becoming disposable waste.

Aluminum Frames

Aluminum Frames provide lightweight, strong support for shelves, gear racks, and sections of the habitat. These frames can become bent, broken, or obsolete when habitats are reconfigured or upgraded, resulting in structural waste that must be managed.

Mounting Brackets

Mounting Brackets are used to attach tools, devices, and equipment securely to walls or structures. They can become damaged, stripped, or unnecessary as the habitat layout changes, turning them into discarded materials that accumulate over time.

Frames & Supports

Frames and supports hold up shelves, gear racks, and sections of the habitat. They help keep things stable in low gravity. Over time, they may bend, break, or become unnecessary after layout changes — turning into rigid waste that can be reused for building or repairs.

Tubes & Rods

Tubes and rods carry air, wires, or help support structures. Plastic tubes may crack or clog, and metal rods can bend or get replaced during upgrades. These leftover parts add to structural waste but can be reshaped or reused for tools and frames.

Plastic Tubes

Plastic Tubes serve to carry air, fluids, or wiring safely throughout the habitat. They may crack, clog, or degrade during use, and when replaced or removed during system upgrades, they add to the volume of plastic waste.

Metal Rods

Metal Rods are used to support equipment, act as handles, or form part of structural frameworks. These rods can become bent, broken, or redundant during repairs and upgrades, contributing to the pile of structural waste that must be creatively reused or recycled.
